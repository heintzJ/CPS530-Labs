Part (a)

Part (a) took a lot longer than Part (b). I had to figure out exactly what column sizes to use and how many to use. I also had to include responsive breakpoints for each of the 3 devices I decided to include. These were mobile, tablet, and desktop. It took much longer to get the elements to appear in the right position. I had to play around with different sizes and borders, and it just took too much time.

Part (b)

Part (b) was much easier with the Bootstrap framework. It automatically creates responsive breakpoints and just makes the page look better. All I had to do was specify the class for each section to be class="col-lg-4 col-xs-4", and the columns are scaled automatically, depending on the size of the window. It also appears to adjust the sizes of elements better, when resizing the window.
