Kuala Lumpur

Where is Kuala Lumpur?

Kuala Lumpur is the capital of Malaysia on the west-central Peninsula.

Population

The population of Kuala Lumur is 8 662 000, making it the 46th largest city in the world.

Petronas Twin Towers

The Petronas Twin Towers (right) are the tallest twin skyscrapers in the world. Construction started in 1993 and the buildings were completed in 1996. The 88 storey buildings stand at 451.9 meters tall and were the tallest buildings in the world until 2004. They were also the tallest buildings in Malaysia until 2019, with the completion of The Exchange 106.

History

Kuala Lumur was founded in 1857, by a group of Chinese tin miners. They founded a settlement at what is now known as the suburb of Ampang. The community flourished, and in 1880, it became the state capital, taking the position from Klang. The growth after is largely attributed to Sir Frank Swettenham, a British resident. He initiated construction of the Klang-Kuala Lumur Railway and encouraged the use of brick and tile in buildings. The city became the capital of the Federated Malay States in 1895.
The city was under Japan occupation during World War II, and was under a communist-led insurgency during 1948-1960. The Federated Malay States became the Federation of Malaya in 1957, and then just Malaysia in 1963. It was and is the capital of both of countries.
